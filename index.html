The complete workflow

One accredited lab, from sample to publication.

Running a microbiome study in-house means sequencing capability, bioinformatics, and the logistics of moving biological samples — infrastructure most research teams don't have, and shouldn't need to build.

We provide the entire workflow as a single service: collection kits and sample pickup, shotgun sequencing, a rigorous analysis pipeline, and a scientist who interprets the results with you. What you receive is a complete, defensible answer, ready to publish.

How it works

Six steps, one point of contact.

STEP 01

Sample collection & logistics

We dispatch barcoded collection kits to your site and arrange sample pickup. Samples are stable at room temperature for up to two weeks — no cold chain required.

STEP 02

Sequencing

Long-read shotgun metagenomic sequencing on Oxford Nanopore, resolved to species level.

STEP 03

Upstream analysis

Quality control, human host-read removal, and taxonomic classification against our in-house curated database of ~250,000 genomes.

STEP 04

Downstream analysis

Normalisation, nine analysis modules, and six-method consensus differential abundance.

STEP 05

Interpretation

A one-to-one session with our scientific team, translating the statistics into biological findings for your study.

STEP 06

Manuscript support

Publication-ready figures and drafting support for your methods and results.

Our approach

We report what six methods agree on.

Every statistical method normalises and tests differently — Mann–Whitney and LEfSe are rank-based, t-test and ANOVA parametric, edgeR and DESeq2 model counts, MaAsLin2 adjusts for covariates. A taxon reaches your results only when at least three agree, each at p < 0.05 and FDR < 0.05. A finding that survives three statistical families is not an artefact of one tool's assumptions.

Study designs

Built around your design, not a template.

We build the comparisons from your metadata. Common designs:

Cohort / cross-sectionalLongitudinal (pre / mid / post)Case–controlMulti-arm interventionAge / sex-stratifiedCustom combinations
Not only stool. In the past year we have run human gut, skin and oral, sheep rumen, poultry caecal digesta, soil rhizosphere, a tribal-cohort study, and Ayurveda trials.

Batch-effect control

Samples are stored and sequenced together at study end, so group differences reflect biology, not sequencing batch.

Covariate adjustment

Age, sex and timepoint are modelled explicitly, so differences trace to the variable of interest.

Sample-size guidance

A minimum of 3–5 per group to run; 15–20 recommended for robust differential abundance. We advise at scoping.

Questions

Frequently asked.

Why shotgun sequencing instead of 16S?
Shotgun sequencing reads all the DNA in a sample, so we identify bacteria, archaea, fungi and viruses to species level, and measure function — pathways and antibiotic-resistance genes. 16S profiles mainly bacteria to about genus level and infers function indirectly.
What is the minimum sample size per group?
The minimum is 3 samples per group. For robust, publication-grade differential abundance we recommend 15–20 per group; smaller cohorts are run and flagged as exploratory. We confirm the right number for your study during scoping.
How long does it take?
Typically two to three weeks from the day we receive your samples. Larger cohorts or additional panels can extend this; a firm timeline is agreed at scoping.
Do you only work with stool samples?
No. Stool is our default, but we have run oral, skin, soil, sheep rumen and poultry samples. Collection and processing are tuned to your sample type.
Can you analyse sequencing data I already have?
Yes. If you have raw shotgun FASTQ reads, we can run the full downstream pipeline with no sequencing required. We confirm your platform first.
Who owns the data, and is it confidential?
You do. Your raw and processed data remain yours to keep, held confidentially, and we work under NDA where required.
